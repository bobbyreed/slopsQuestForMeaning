#### Slop's Quest for Meaning: Chapter 2 Visual Dev Log

*Date: April 29, 2026*

##### Process Summary

This document captures the visual development session for Chapter 2 of Slop's Quest for Meaning. Building off the Zelda I → II shift established in the game design document, the goal was to pivot from the top-down beige overworld of Chapter 1 into a side-scrolling, gravity-bound platformer environment. 

The core aesthetic directive was to visualize a "desert wasteland of digital stuff, memory leaks, and bits of randomly generated art." To achieve this, the generative process focused on fragmenting familiar assets into data slop:

* **Slop (The Protagonist):** Transitioned from a simple 2-frame legacy blob into a fully articulated sprite with a wireframe/data-glitch body, while maintaining the recognizable lavender head. Movement sets were expanded to include jumps, dashes, and melee strikes.
* **The Bestiary:** Chapter 1's architectural remnants were reimagined as "Geners" and data-ghosts—malicious routines, uncleaned memory addresses, and syntax manifestations.
* **The World:** The environment tilesets evolved from basic rock formations into decaying cloud infrastructure, executable floors, and corrupted file blocks. Background concepts ranged from parched data deserts to subterranean data caverns and, ultimately, the overgrown ruins of a decayed cloud app.
